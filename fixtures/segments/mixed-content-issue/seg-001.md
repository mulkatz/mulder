# Editorial: The State of UFO Research in 2024

The year 2024 marks a turning point for the study of unidentified anomalous phenomena. Congressional hearings, whistleblower testimony, and the establishment of the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) have brought unprecedented institutional legitimacy to a field long marginalized by mainstream science.

## A New Institutional Framework

The National Defense Authorization Act of 2023 mandated that AARO compile a historical review of UAP-related government programs. This represents the first time the U.S. government has formally acknowledged the need for a comprehensive accounting of its own involvement in UAP research.

## Challenges Ahead

Despite these advances, significant obstacles remain. The stigma associated with reporting UAP encounters continues to deter military pilots and other credentialed observers from coming forward. The scientific community, while increasingly open to the topic, still lacks standardized protocols for investigating aerial anomalies.

As we enter 2025, this publication remains committed to rigorous, evidence-based reporting on all aspects of the phenomenon.
