# Rendlesham Forest Incident: Official MOD Report

## Summary

In the early morning hours of December 26, 1980, personnel from RAF Woodbridge observed unusual lights in Rendlesham Forest, Suffolk, England. Deputy Base Commander Lieutenant Colonel Charles Halt led a team into the forest to investigate.

## Witness Accounts

Staff Sergeant Jim Penniston was among the first to approach the unidentified craft. He described a triangular object approximately three meters across, resting on the forest floor. Penniston reported touching the craft and noted hieroglyphic-like markings on its surface.

Airman First Class John Burroughs accompanied Penniston and confirmed the presence of an unusual light source. Both men reported experiencing disorientation and a sense of static electricity.

## Physical Evidence

Radiation readings taken at the landing site by Colonel Halt showed levels significantly above background. Three circular depressions were found in the frozen ground, consistent with landing gear impressions. Broken tree branches above the site suggested an object had descended through the canopy.

## Official Response

The Ministry of Defence concluded that the incident posed no threat to national security. The Halt Memo, dispatched to the MOD on January 13, 1981, remains the primary official document acknowledging the event. Subsequent FOIA releases have revealed additional internal correspondence questioning the adequacy of the official investigation.
