# Inside Project Blue Book: Declassified Case Files

The United States Air Force officially closed Project Blue Book in December 1969, concluding that no UFO reported, investigated, and evaluated posed a threat to national security. However, recently declassified documents tell a more nuanced story.

## Case #10073: The Malmstrom AFB Incident

On March 16, 1967, ten Minuteman ICBMs at Echo Flight, Malmstrom Air Force Base, Montana, simultaneously went offline. Captain Robert Salas, the on-duty missile commander, reported a glowing red object hovering above the front gate.

The official Blue Book explanation listed the incident as "equipment malfunction," but internal Air Force memos obtained through FOIA reveal that investigators could not identify a technical cause for the simultaneous failures.

## Case #14562: The Tehran Incident

On September 19, 1976, Iranian Air Force F-4 Phantoms were scrambled to intercept a brilliant light over Tehran. Both aircraft experienced instrumentation failures when approaching the object. The pilot of the lead aircraft, General Parviz Jafari, later confirmed these details to American investigators.

## The Robertson Panel Legacy

These cases highlight the gap between Blue Book's public conclusions and its classified assessments. The CIA-sponsored Robertson Panel of 1953 had recommended that UFO reports be "debunked" to reduce public interest, a directive that shaped Blue Book's approach for its remaining 16 years.
