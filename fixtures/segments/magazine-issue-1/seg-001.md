# The Phoenix Lights Revisited: New Witness Testimony

On the evening of March 13, 1997, thousands of witnesses across Arizona reported seeing a massive V-shaped formation of lights traversing the sky from the Nevada border to Tucson. Nearly three decades later, new witnesses have come forward with previously unreported observations.

## The Henderson Testimony

Maria Henderson, a retired airline pilot living in Prescott Valley, kept silent about her observation for over 25 years. "I knew what I was seeing was not conventional aircraft," she told our investigators. "The object was completely silent and moved at a speed inconsistent with any known aircraft."

Henderson's account is notable because of her professional background. As a former commercial pilot with over 15,000 flight hours, her ability to identify aircraft is well-established.

## Radar Data Anomalies

Newly obtained FOIA documents from Luke Air Force Base reveal that radar operators tracked an anomalous return moving southeast at approximately 400 knots. The track disappeared near the Estrella Mountains at 20:28 MST.

Dr. Richard Haines, former NASA research scientist, analyzed the radar data: "The track is consistent with a single large object, not a formation of separate vehicles."

## Photographic Evidence

Amateur photographer James Dalton captured a sequence of 35mm photographs from his backyard in Tempe. Digital enhancement by the National Institute for Discovery Science (NIDS) revealed structural details not visible in the original prints.
